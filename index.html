I am currently a visiting research scholar at Mechanical Systems Control Lab, UC Berkeley, supervised by Prof. Masayoshi Tomizuka. I am also a senior student at Renmin University of China major in Computer Science. My interest mainly falls on reinforcement learning and machine learning. My goal is to incorporate learning algorithms in traditional control tasks while achieving robustness and able to generalize well.
Before coming to UC Berkeley, I was a research intern at Intelligent Control Lab, Carnegie Mellon University, and fortunately to be advised by Prof. Changliu Liu.
Interests. Deep Reinforcement Learning, Machine Learning, Computer Vision

Projects

  • AutoEnv, an integrated platform for autonomous driving simulation [code] [pdf] This platform includes trajectory and lane data preprocessing, physical simulation, algorithm implementation and evaluation.
  • VioTrace, a system for violence detection and tracking [code] We design a system named VioTrace to assist people in detecting violence behavior and tracking criminals in a multi-channel camera scenario. This system is consisted of two parts, violence detection and human re-identification. For the first part, we use a C3D network to detect the violence scene from a stream of video. During the second phase, we first implement YOLO v2 to give the bounding box of the person in the scene, then do human re-identification for multi-channel camera to track the person involved in the violence incident.
  • RealU, a web application for entrepreneurs preparing for their career [code] An independent developed web application using flask. The motivation for this project is completely out of personal interest and want to practise for large scale programming as well.
